Brand Strategy & Visual Identity

Riwaaz — enduring grace, perpetual heritage.

A creative brief and full brand identity system for a modern luxury jewellery house rooted in South Asian artistry, heirloom quality, and responsible sourcing.

Role

Brand strategy, market research, creative direction, visual identity

Deliverables

Positioning, personas, moodboard, logo lockup, colour & type system

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, Figma

Download full creative brief (PDF) ↓
Riwaaz

01 — Brand foundation

A tribute to tradition, skill, and opulence

Riwaaz needed to be more than another jewellery label — the brief called for a brand that treated South Asian jewellery-making traditions (polki, kundan, meenakari) as the foundation of a modern luxury identity, not a decorative afterthought.

"Enduring grace, perpetual heritage."

02 — Strategy & competition

Finding the gap

A landscape review of Tanishq, Kalyan Jewellers, Hazoorilal Legacy, and Cartier surfaced a clear opening: broad appeal, or bridal focus, or international prestige — never all three, and never with real cultural depth.

03 — Customer personas

Three women, one house

Rather than design for a single buyer, the brief mapped three distinct relationships to fine jewellery — bridal heirloom, established collector, and everyday independence — so the identity would flex across occasions without diluting itself.

04 — Visual identity

Moodboard

Gold leaf textures, bridal bangles, and jewel-toned fabric swatches set the tactile direction — opulent without tipping into costume.

04 — Visual identity

Colour & type

A deep wine and gold primary palette carries the weight of heritage; Playfair Display and Inter give the system an editorial, trustworthy voice on web and print alike.

04 — Visual identity

Icons & interface elements

A small icon set — ring, gem, earrings, bracelet, necklace — plus three button states, gives the identity a starting point for e-commerce and digital application.

05 — From brand to interface

A live, clickable prototype

The palette, type, and icon system carried straight through into a full hi-fi interactive prototype — click through the shopping experience on desktop and mobile below.
